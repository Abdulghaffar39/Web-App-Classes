FAQ

  • A drawing can be defined as a visual representation of objects, concepts, emotions, and even abstract forms. Although that’s a very broad definition, drawing is considered graphic art, and it emphasizes form or shape rather than mass and color. Drawing, consequently, comes in many types. Depending on the source or author, the number and the names of these styles may vary. Some of the most common are cartoon drawing, figure drawing, gesture drawing, line drawing, and perspective drawing, but these are just a few.

  • There are five skills that are considered basic in terms of drawing as an art, but, once again, this may vary depending on the author or source. Recognizing edges, understanding proportions and perspectives, applying color and understanding what each one means, using lighting and shadows effectively, and (perhaps the most important one) communicating what you want to the viewer.

  • Something as broad as popularity varies depending on many circumstances, such as demand, culture, location, purpose, etc. Here are some that are considered more widespread than others (but note that there are many more): Cartoon, Pointillism, Line drawing, Photorealism, Architectural drawing, etc.

  • Drawing is a visual art form in which the artist uses lines to represent objects, characters, animals, or even concepts. It’s created with the so-called dry media: pencils, chalk, charcoal, etc. Meanwhile, painting focuses more on color and forms. It’s mostly created with paint (hence the name), and the types of surfaces used are broader than in drawing, which usually focuses on paper or canvas.
